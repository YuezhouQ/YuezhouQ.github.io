Research

I am broadly interested in variable selection, selective inference, high-dimensional statistics, time-series/functional data, causal inference, machine learning, and applications to scientific problems such as neuro-imaging.

Selective inference for Gaussian graphical models

Undergraduate Research Program · University of Michigan · 2024–Present
Advisor: Prof. Snigdha Panigrahi

I study selective inference procedures for Gaussian graphical models, implementing randomized and conditional approaches to quantify uncertainty after model selection. I run simulation studies across graph structures to understand coverage–power trade-offs and evaluate de-biasing and post-selection interval strategies under sparsity misspecification.

Probabilistic models for space-weather forecasting

Undergraduate Research Program · University of Michigan · 2023–2023
Advisor: Prof. Yang Chen

I work on forecasting geomagnetic perturbations and geomagnetically induced currents using NASA space-weather datasets. The project combines time-series features with machine learning and sparse variational contaminated-noise Gaussian process regression, benchmarked against artificial neural network baselines on held-out temporal splits.

Monte Carlo simulation of the St. Petersburg game

STATS 506 · R, Monte Carlo, robust estimation

Implemented an R package to simulate the St. Petersburg game, study heavy-tailed payoffs with infinite expectation, and visualize the normalized average \( A_n = \frac{1}{n \log_2 n} \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \) under repeated sampling.
